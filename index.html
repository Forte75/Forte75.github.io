Narcotic Crime Data Analysis · San Francisco

How to fight the recent fentanyl crisis in San Francisco?

By Maria Vendas & Diogo Forte · Social Data Analysis · April 2026

San Francisco is currently undergoing a fentanyl crisis, as many other cities in the United States. A crime data analysis revealed that the city has suffered a previous drug crisis and was able to mitigate the issue. Could we use previous data on drug/narcotics crime to fight this new epidemic?

Section 01

Dataset Overview and Methodology

To understand where San Francisco stands today, you first have to know where it has been. We pulled two open records from the SF Police Department — stitched together into a single, unbroken timeline that stretches across more than two decades of street-level data.

22 years of continuous crime data, from 2003 to 2025

Each incident in both datasets carries the same essential details: the type of offence, the date and time it was reported, and the exact district where it occurred. That consistency is what makes the comparison possible — and what makes the drug numbers so striking.

2 datasets merged on crime category, district, date, and coordinates

Both records are published on SF OpenData and are freely accessible to the public. The older file covers 2003 to 2018; the newer one picks up from 2018 through 2025, giving us a rare, long-range lens on how one city's relationship with drugs has shifted over time.
